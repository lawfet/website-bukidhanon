The Second Highest Peak in the Philippines - A Majestic Crown of Mindanao

Mount Dulang-Dulang: The Philippines' Second Highest Summit

Elevation: 2,938 meters above sea level ‚Ä¢ Difficulty: Very Challenging ‚Ä¢ Location: Kitanglad Mountain Range, Bukidnon

Introduction

Mount Dulang-Dulang stands majestically as the second highest mountain in the Philippines, reaching an impressive elevation of 2,938 meters above sea level. Located in the heart of the Kitanglad Mountain Range in Bukidnon, this peak is not only a geographical giant but also a sacred place for the indigenous peoples of the region and a critical biodiversity hotspot.

Often referred to simply as "D2" by mountaineers, Dulang-Dulang forms part of the Kitanglad Mountain Range Natural Park, a protected area that has been designated as an ASEAN Heritage Park. The mountain's name means "place of continuous lightning" in the local language, reflecting the often-dramatic weather conditions that characterize its summit.

Geographical Significance

As the second highest peak in the Philippines, Mount Dulang-Dulang is surpassed only by Mount Apu (2,954 meters). However, many climbers consider D2 to be equally challenging and rewarding, with its own unique characteristics that make it a must-conquer peak for serious mountaineers.

Key Facts:

  • Rank: 2nd highest mountain in the Philippines
  • Elevation: 2,938 meters above sea level
  • Location: Kitanglad Mountain Range, Bukidnon Province
  • Mountain Range: Part of the Kitanglad Range which includes 5 major peaks
  • Protected Status: ASEAN Heritage Park and National Park

Cultural and Spiritual Importance

Mount Dulang-Dulang holds deep cultural and spiritual significance for the indigenous peoples of Bukidnon, particularly the Talaandig, Higaonon, and Bukidnon tribes. The mountain is considered a sacred place where spirits dwell and where traditional rituals are performed to seek guidance and blessings.

Before climbing, visitors are often required to participate in traditional ceremonies led by tribal elders or local guides. These rituals include offerings to the mountain spirits and prayers for safe passage. This cultural aspect adds depth and meaning to the climbing experience, connecting adventurers to the rich heritage of the region.

The Climbing Challenge

Climbing Mount Dulang-Dulang is a serious undertaking that requires proper preparation, physical fitness, and respect for the mountain's demands. The typical climb takes 2-3 days, with overnight camping at designated campsites.

Climbing Overview:

  • Day 1: Jump-off to Camp 1 (6-8 hours trekking)
  • Day 2: Camp 1 to Summit and back to Camp 1 (8-10 hours)
  • Day 3: Camp 1 to Jump-off (3-4 hours)
  • Total Distance: Approximately 15-20 kilometers round trip
  • Elevation Gain: Nearly 2,000 meters from base to summit

Trail Experience

The journey to Mount Dulang-Dulang's summit is a progression through distinct ecological zones, each offering unique experiences and challenges:

Trail Sections:

  • Lowland Forest: Starting elevation with agricultural lands and secondary forest
  • Montane Forest: Transition zone with towering dipterocarp trees
  • Mossy Forest: Enchanting high-elevation forest with epiphytes and thick moss
  • Alpine Zone: Near-summit area with dwarf vegetation and exposed ridges
  • Summit Ridge: Final approach to the peak with panoramic views

Natural Wonders and Biodiversity

Mount Dulang-Dulang is part of one of the most biodiverse regions in the Philippines. The mountain and surrounding forests provide habitat for numerous species, many of which are endemic and endangered.

Wildlife Highlights:

  • Philippine Eagle: The national bird and one of the world's largest eagles
  • Mindanao Scops Owl: Endemic owl species found in the mossy forests
  • Philippine Warty Pig: Endemic wild pig species
  • Various Hornbills: Multiple hornbill species including the rare writhed hornbill
  • Endemic Plants: Numerous orchid, fern, and tree species

The Summit Experience

Reaching the summit of Mount Dulang-Dulang is an achievement that few accomplish. The peak offers a 360-degree panoramic view that stretches across Mindanao, including views of other major peaks in the Kitanglad range.

The summit area is characterized by dwarf vegetation and rocky outcrops. On clear days, climbers can see as far as Mount Apu to the south and other mountain ranges across Mindanao. The feeling of standing on the second highest point in the Philippines is indescribable - a mixture of exhaustion, exhilaration, and profound respect for nature's grandeur.

Weather and Climate

The weather on Mount Dulang-Dulang is unpredictable and can change rapidly. Due to its high elevation, the mountain experiences significantly cooler temperatures than the lowlands, with summit temperatures often dropping to near-freezing levels.

Weather Patterns:

  • Temperature Range: 5¬∞C to 20¬∞C depending on elevation and season
  • Rainfall: High rainfall throughout the year, heaviest during monsoon season
  • Fog: Frequent fog and low clouds, especially in the mossy forest zone
  • Winds: Strong winds at the summit, requiring proper gear

Best Time to Climb

The ideal climbing period for Mount Dulang-Dulang is during the dry season from December to May. However, even during these months, climbers should be prepared for rain and changing weather conditions.

February to April typically offers the best combination of relatively dry weather and clear skies, providing the best chance for summit views. However, these are also the most popular months, so early booking of guides and permits is essential.

Practical Information

Permits and Regulations:

  • Required climbing permits from the Department of Environment and Natural Resources
  • Local guide mandatory (usually from indigenous communities)
  • Maximum group size restrictions apply
  • Environmental fee and guide fees required
  • Registration at the jump-off point mandatory

Essential Equipment:

  • Four-season tent or high-quality mountain tent
  • Sleeping bag rated for cold weather (0¬∞C or lower)
  • Waterproof hiking boots with ankle support
  • Rain gear (jacket and pants)
  • Layered clothing system including thermal wear
  • Headlamp with spare batteries
  • Trekking poles for stability
  • First aid kit with emergency supplies
  • Water purification system
  • GPS device or satellite communicator

Jump-off Points:

The primary jump-off point is in Barangay Songco, Lantapan, Bukidnon. Most climbers arrange transportation from Malaybalay City or Cagayan de Oro City. The journey to the jump-off point typically takes 2-3 hours from major urban centers.

Conservation and Environmental Protection

Mount Dulang-Dulang is a critical conservation area, serving as habitat for numerous endangered species and as an important watershed for surrounding communities. The mountain is part of the Kitanglad Mountain Range Natural Park, which is managed to protect its biodiversity and cultural values.

Climbers are expected to follow strict environmental guidelines, including proper waste disposal, staying on designated trails, and respecting wildlife. The mountain's protected status means that certain areas may be off-limits to protect fragile ecosystems.

Conclusion

Mount Dulang-Dulang represents the pinnacle of Philippine mountaineering challenges outside of Luzon. Its combination of height, biodiversity, cultural significance, and raw natural beauty makes it one of the most rewarding climbing destinations in the country.

The journey to its summit is more than just a physical challenge; it's a spiritual and cultural experience that connects climbers to the deep traditions of Mindanao's indigenous peoples and to the fragile beauty of one of the Philippines' most important natural treasures. Standing on its summit, surrounded by clouds and endless views, you understand why this mountain has earned its place among the great peaks of the Philippines.

For those who accept its challenge, Mount Dulang-Dulang offers not just the satisfaction of conquering the country's second highest peak, but a profound appreciation for the importance of conservation, cultural respect, and the enduring power of nature to inspire and humble the human spirit.
